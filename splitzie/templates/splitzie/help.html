Q&A

How to enter a starting balance?

A way to enter a starting balance is to create a payment with amount 0 and type income. Then enter the starting balances in the division fields, with negative balances as a negative value.

Entering a payment in this way will modify the balances exactly as inputted in the division fields.

What can you use custom repayments for?

The repayment form on the Settle page lets you enter arbitrary repayments. For example, if you owe 2 euros to Bob and 3 euros to Alice, instead of repaying both of them, you could also give 5 euros to Bob and ask him to pay the remainder forward.

Enter the 5 euros repayment into the form, and the balances will change so that Bob now owes 3 euros to Alice.

How are division amounts rounded?

When a payment is divided, the division amounts may need to be rounded. This means that some participants will get assigned an amount that is one cent higher than other participants, to make sure that the sum of all divisions is exactly equal to the total expense amount. The participants who pay this extra cent are chosen randomly. The difference is always at most one cent.

For nerds: you can view the division script in the page source code (/static/splitzie.js).

About

This app is a hobby project, created because I was annoyed that the widely used Dutch apps WieBetaaltWat and Groepie do not allow for partial settlements (between a subset of participants), and that they want you to link your bank account for settling.

This app will remain online and free as long as it is being used. The source code may be made open source if there is interest in that.

To contact me, send an e-mail to mail@maartenvisscher.nl.

Privacy

This website does not use tracking and session cookies and does not log IP addresses. There is no account system.

Security

Anyone with the link of a group can edit the group. If your group link was stolen, create a new group.
