Q&A

{% translate 'How to enter a starting balance?' %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} To enter a starting balance, create a payment with amount 0 and type income. Enter the starting balances in the division fields, with negative balances as a negative value. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} Entering a payment in this way will modify the balances exactly as inputted in the division fields. {% endblocktrans %}

{% translate 'When would you enter a custom repayment amount?' %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} The repayment form on the settle page lets you enter arbitrary repayment amounts. For example, if you owe 2 euros to Bob and 3 euros to Alice, instead of repaying both of them, you could also give 5 euros to Bob and ask him to pay the remainder forward. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} Enter the 5 euros repayment into the form, and the balances will change so that Bob now owes 3 euros to Alice. {% endblocktrans %}

{% translate 'How are division amounts rounded?' %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} When a payment is divided, the division amounts may need to be rounded. This means that some participants will get assigned an amount that is one cent higher than other participants, to make sure that the sum of all divisions is exactly equal to the total expense amount. The participants who pay this extra cent are chosen randomly. {% endblocktrans %}
